Mysterious Pokémon types are Mythical Pokémon (rare event-exclusive 'mons like Mew, Celebi, Arceus) or the idea of new types (like "Cosmic"), but also include gameplay oddities, such as Psyduck's hidden Psychic power or early-game glitches/unidentified Pokémon. Some types themselves have mysteries, like how Ghost-type was weak to Psychic in Gen 1, or the origins of Paradox Pokémon like Great Tusk, which seem connected to ancient times. Types of Pokémon Mysteries Mythical Pokémon: Extremely rare Pokémon obtained through special events, embodying myth and legend (e.g., Mew, Jirachi, Arceus, Zarude). Paradox Pokémon: Ancient or future versions of existing Pokémon (like Great Tusk/Donphan) found in Area Zero, linked to Terapagos and time travel. Unidentified/Glitched Pokémon: Early Pokémon manga featured unnamed or strange-looking Pokémon, like a Slowbro with a Gligar's head, that never became official. Hidden Potential: Pokémon like Psyduck have lore suggesting untapped powers (Psychic) that only manifest under certain conditions (headaches), making their typing seem strange initially. Unused/Fan-Made Types: Concepts like a "Cosmic" type have been proposed, with Pokémon like Solrock or Necrozma fitting the theme, though they received existing types instead. Mysterious Pokémon by Category Mythical Pokémon: Mew, Celebi, Jirachi, Deoxys, Darkrai, Arceus, Zeraora, Zarude, Pecharunt. Legendary Pokémon (often confused with Mythical): Lugia, Ho-Oh, Kyogre, Groudon, Rayquaza, Dialga, Palkia, Giratina, Reshiram, Zekrom, Xerneas, Yveltal, Zygarde, Solgaleo, Lunala, Eternatus, Koraidon, Miraidon. Specific Lore Mysteries: Arceus: Can change between all 18 types. Shaymin: Has a form change between Land and Sky Formes. Type: Null/Silvally: Created to counter Ultra Beasts, can hold different types. Treasures of Ruin (Paldea): Chi-Yu, Chien-Pao, Ting-Lu, Wo-Chien are linked to ancient cursed treasures. Old Type Mysteries Gen 1 Psychic Power: Psychic types were immune to Ghost-type moves and resisted Poison, making them overpowered. Bug vs. Poison: In Gen 1, Bug was weak to Poison, but Poison was not super-effective against Bug, which was later changed. These mysterious Pokémon and type interactions add depth and intrigue to the Pokémon world, with some mysteries resolved and new ones always appearing with each new game!.
